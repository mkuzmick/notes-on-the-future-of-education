Connection to the Broader Project — Harvard's Oral Assessment Diversity
Surveying Harvard's own oral traditions across 9 cycles reveals tremendous variation by school and discipline:
Oral-intensive: GSD (daily desk crits), Law (Socratic method), Medicine (rounds), Kennedy (Spring Exercise), Radcliffe (fellows' talks), some College concentrations (Gen Exams, thesis defenses)
Oral-minimal: Extension School, many College concentrations, most lecture courses
The pattern: Oral assessment thrives where it aligns with professional practice (designers critique, lawyers argue, doctors present cases) or institutional mission (Radcliffe fosters interdisciplinary exchange). It's absent where accessibility or scale requires different formats.

Bachelor of Liberal Arts Degree Requirements — Harvard Extension School

The ALB degree requires 128 total credits, with a minimum of 64 credits completed at Harvard (typically 16 courses). Students may transfer up to 64 credits from other institutions.

Core Requirements:
- 40 credits in their chosen concentration, with at least 32 from Harvard
- 16-credit distribution requirement outside their concentration (8 credits in each of two other areas)
- 12 expository/speech credits at Harvard, including required EXPO 25
- 4-8 foreign language credits (varies by option)
- 4 quantitative reasoning credits at Harvard
- 4 moral reasoning credits at Harvard

"Of the 128 required credits, 52 credits are taken with Harvard instructors and 60 credits need to be from upper-level coursework."

On-Campus Experience:
The degree is "offered nearly entirely online, with just eight credits on campus." Students choose between accelerated options (two or four weekends) or standard summer courses to fulfill this requirement.

Grade Requirements:
Core requirements need C- or higher; EXPO 25 and preadmission courses require B or higher. Field of study and minor courses require B- or higher. Students must maintain a cumulative 2.0 GPA after admission.
