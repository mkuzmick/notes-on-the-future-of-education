Elle's notes:
The Latin viva voce means "the living voice." This framing positions oral examination as a more dynamic assessment where candidates can "rebut arguments, clarify confusions and ambiguities, take advice for improvements, and get developmental feedback." The paper distinguishes between 'viva', 'oral defence', 'thesis defence', and 'oral examination' as terms with potentially different pedagogical purposes.

Why it matters: The "living voice" etymology is compelling for Harvard's framing—it positions oral assignments not as a retreat from AI but as a return to something more authentically human and dialogic.
