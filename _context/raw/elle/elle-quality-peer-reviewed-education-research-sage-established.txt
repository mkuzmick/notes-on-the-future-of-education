Summary: Management consulting firms (McKinsey, BCG, Bain) have developed highly standardized oral presentation formats for client briefings: slide-driven, data-heavy, executive-summary-first, action-oriented. Training emphasizes "simplifying complexity, highlighting what matters, and persuading under pressure." Consultants learn to present to CEOs with limited time, anticipate objections, and structure recommendations using pyramid principles. Hundreds of real consulting presentations are publicly available as training resources.

Aspiration: many Harvard graduates enter consulting; teaching this communication style serves professional preparation (Economics, Government, Business concentrations could incorporate consulting-style briefings). Warning: the McKinsey aesthetic — slick, polished, data-driven confidence — is precisely what the earlier NYU professor detected in AI-generated student work ("McKinsey memos"). If Harvard oral exams reward consulting-style presentations, we may inadvertently privilege: (1) Performance over substance — confident delivery masking shallow analysis; (2) Corporate communication norms — privileging efficiency and persuasion over nuance and doubt; (3) Cultural capital — students from business backgrounds or with internship experience have unfair advantage. The synthesis: Harvard should teach consulting communication transparently (here's a professional genre, here's why it works, here's its limitations) while valuing other oral forms (contemplative dialogue, exploratory thinking-aloud, collaborative brainstorming).
Source quality: Professional training resources (Udemy), not academic research. Represent industry standards but aren't pedagogically neutral.

Description
Why do CEOs pay consultancies hundreds of thousands to make a few slides and give a few presentations? 

Besides performing in-depth analysis, consultants synthesize hundreds of hours of research into a structured, compelling presentation that fits within 30 minutes to 1 hour. Would you like to bring this level of professionalism and polish to all your presentations at work? 

This course gives you the secrets used by top-tier consulting firms like McKinsey and BCG to present to CEOs and other senior clients. It is based on the first-hand experience of two consultants (ex-McKinsey and ex-BCG). Together, we have consolidated our experiences into one easy-to-follow course. This is a concise, to-the-point course summarizes for you the most important best practices for preparing and giving a presentation, alongside visual examples of good versus bad presentations. 

Equipped wtih these tips, you will stand out from colleagues, impress your stakeholders, and set yourself up for success in your role. This is applicable for anyone who needs to give a presentation, including students, potential consultants, Business Analysts, Data Analysts, Project Managers, Strategy and Corporate development roles (and many more!).

By the end of the course, you will understand:

- How to structure a presentation

- How to design slides to share quantitative and qualitative ideas

- How to present confidently and professionally

Who this course is for:
- Anyone who may need to give a presentation (consultants, business analysts, corporate strategy, project managers, and more)
