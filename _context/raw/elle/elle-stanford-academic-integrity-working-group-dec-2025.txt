Stanford's Academic Integrity Working Group: AI and Exam Policies

Stanford's Academic Integrity Working Group (AIWG) has released updated guidance for faculty regarding generative AI use and exam administration as the university enters its third year of a proctoring pilot program.

Proctoring Pilot Expansion:
The AIWG launched its multi-year pilot study in spring 2024 with seven courses. Participation has grown significantly, with over 50 courses across multiple schools now engaged in the pilot during the current quarter.

AI Detection Concerns:
The working group identified substantial limitations with AI detection tools, noting they suffer from "bias and occurrences of false positives and negatives." The group determined these tools "often fail to reliably assess the extent of AI involvement" in mixed human-AI content, making them unsuitable for high-stakes academic misconduct cases.

Recommendations for Faculty:
For courses not in the proctoring pilot, the AIWG recommends "in-person formats such as oral exams and in-class writing assignments" when faculty wish to restrict AI use during evaluations.

Departmental Policy Development:
The AIWG emphasizes that "departments and multi-section courses are encouraged to establish consistent and enforceable AI policies." Such policies should be clearly communicated and regularly discussed with students throughout the academic year.
