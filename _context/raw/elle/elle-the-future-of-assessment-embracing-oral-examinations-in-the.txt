As artificial intelligence (AI) continues to revolutionize various sectors, education stands at a pivotal crossroads (again). AI tools that can generate essays, solve complex equations, or translate languages are readily available to students. While these technologies offer incredible learning opportunities, they also present challenges in academic integrity and assessment authenticity. One innovative solution gaining traction is the use of oral examinations in middle and high schools across various subjects. This approach not only mitigates the risk of AI-assisted cheating but also provides a more accurate gauge of a student's understanding.

The Challenge of AI in Education
The advent of AI has made information and solutions accessible at the click of a button. Students can now bypass the learning process by using AI to generate answers for assignments and exams. This ease of access undermines traditional assessment methods, making it difficult for educators to discern genuine understanding from AI-assisted responses.

Oral examinations require students to articulate their knowledge verbally, engaging in a real-time dialogue that AI cannot replicate. This method compels students to try and demonstrate understanding as verbal explanations reveal the depth of a student's comprehension. It helps students think critically with spontaneous questioning it tests the ability to apply concepts. With oral communicate Effectively: Builds essential soft skills like public speaking and active listening.

So what about implementation across subjects? Where and when could we use it? 

Language Arts

- Literary Analysis: Students discuss themes, character development, and stylistic devices in texts.

- Creative Expression: Oral storytelling or poetry recitations showcase interpretive skills.

Mathematics

- Problem-Solving Process: Explaining each step in solving equations ensures conceptual clarity.

- Theoretical Concepts: Discussing the 'why' behind formulas and theorems.

Science

- Experiment Explanations: Describing methodologies and results of experiments.

- Conceptual Discussions: Debating scientific theories and their real-world applications.

Social Studies

- Historical Debates: Arguing different perspectives on historical events.

- Civic Engagement: Discussing current events and their societal impact.

To accommodate larger classes and diverse schedules, oral exams can be recorded and transcribed. This method allows teachers to evaluate performances at their convenience, enabling remote assessment and flexibility. Exams can be conducted by proctors or invigilators, reducing scheduling conflicts and ensuring that assessments can proceed smoothly even when the primary teacher is unavailable. The transcriptions provide a reference point for educators to offer detailed, constructive feedback, enhancing the learning experience and allowing for a thorough review of each student's performance.

Strengths of Oral Examinations
One of the primary strengths of oral examinations is their ability to provide authentic assessment. Through personal interaction, teachers can directly engage with students, which helps in assessing soft skills and building confidence. This direct engagement allows educators to probe deeper into topics as needed, offering immediate clarification and exploring the extent of a student's knowledge.

Oral examinations also bolster academic integrity. Conducted live, they make it difficult for students to use AI tools or other forms of cheating during the assessment. This environment encourages original responses and unique perspectives, ensuring that the student's work is genuinely their own.

In terms of skill development, oral exams enhance students' communication abilities by improving their articulation and presentation skills. They promote critical thinking, requiring students to think quickly and reason under pressure during spontaneous questioning. This format helps students develop the ability to organize their thoughts coherently and express them clearly, which are invaluable skills beyond the classroom.

Additionally, oral assessments offer adaptability by being inclusive of different learning styles. They benefit students who express themselves better verbally, providing an alternative to traditional written exams and catering to a more diverse student population.

Weaknesses and Challenges
Despite their advantages, oral examinations present certain challenges. Student anxiety is a significant concern; the performance pressure associated with speaking in front of others can intimidate some students, potentially affecting their performance. To mitigate this, educators can provide practice sessions and strive to create a supportive environment that helps students feel more comfortable during the assessment.

Oral exams can also be resource-intensive. Conducting individual assessments is time-consuming, especially for large classes. However, utilizing recordings and scheduling efficiently can help manage time constraints, making the process more practical for educators.

Subjectivity in grading is another challenge. Personal impressions may influence evaluations, leading to potential bias. To counteract this, it's important to develop clear rubrics and standardized criteria to ensure fairness and consistency in assessments.

Accessibility concerns must also be considered. Students with speech and hearing impairments may be disadvantaged by oral examinations. To accommodate these students, educators should offer alternative assessment methods when necessary, ensuring that all students have an equal opportunity to demonstrate their understanding.

To sum up, in an educational landscape increasingly influenced by AI, oral examinations offer a promising avenue to maintain academic integrity and assess true understanding. By requiring students to express their knowledge verbally, educators can circumvent the challenges posed by AI-generated answers. While there are hurdles to overcome, such as potential student anxiety and resource demands, the benefits of authentic assessment and skill development are substantial.

Recording and transcribing oral exams further enhance this method's practicality, allowing for flexible scheduling and thorough evaluations. As we adapt to the evolving role of technology in education, incorporating oral examinations could play a crucial role in fostering genuine learning and preparing students for future success.

I believe that embracing oral assessments is not just a reactive measure against AI misuse but a proactive step towards enriching the educational experience. By integrating oral examinations thoughtfully, we teachers can navigate the challenges of modern technology while cultivating a deeper, more authentic learning environment for all students.
