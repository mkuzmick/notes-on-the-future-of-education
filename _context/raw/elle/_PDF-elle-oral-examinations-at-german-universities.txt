German universities maintain oral examination traditions especially for final exams and PhD defenses. ResearchGate (2001) notes: "In Europe, as well as some other parts of the world, oral exams in STEM are not so uncommon, but their usage has been on the decline." Reddit discussions (2021-2025) document that "in smaller Universities, not so much, but in smaller, more specialised subjects, the Students and Professors get in touch earlier and oral exams also play a role." Italy held five oral exams per day during COVID (Sky News 2020), with "one hour allotted for each and a 15-minute break between." German PhD programs typically have "no qualifying exam" (Academia Stack Exchange)—students admitted are considered qualified, then "Your next exam will be handing in your thesis and defending it." The defense "can include an oral exam on the general field of your thesis."

Harvard relevance: European universities never fully abandoned oral assessment the way American institutions did (except French colles, Theme 0 Cycle 3, which intensified it). Germany's continuation of oral exams in STEM is particularly relevant—if engineering and sciences can assess orally, any field can. The COVID-era Italian example (five 1-hour oral exams/day) shows logistical feasibility under crisis conditions: if Italian universities could conduct all-oral assessment during a pandemic, Harvard can do it in normal times. The German PhD model—no quals, just thesis defense—mirrors Theme 0 Cycle 3's finding that academia requires oral defense at the highest level. European continuity contrasts with American abandonment, suggesting the shift to written-only assessment was culturally/institutionally specific, not pedagogically necessary.
